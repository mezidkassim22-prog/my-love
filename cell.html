A cell is basic, smallest unit of life that makes up all living organisms. It is a self-
structure that includes a membrane, cytoplasm, and DNA for genetic material.
Cells perform all the essential functions of life, such as taking in nutrients, converting
them to energy, and reproducing.

Key components and function

Cell membrane: A protective outer boundary that controls what enters and exits the
cell.

Cytoplasm: A jelly-like substance filling the cell, containing various organelles.

DNA: The genetic material that carries the instructions for the cell's functions and is
passed on during reproduction.

organelles: Specialized structures within the cell that perform specific tasks, like the
nucleus (containing theOrgane DNA in eukaryotic cells) and mitochondria (for energy
production).
